Serge Wabeya

Serge Wabeya is the visionary Chairman of the Congo Industrial Consortium and CEO of both Mayele Solutions and Maximum Solutions. With a background in Agricultural Economics and a Master Certificate in Supply Chain Management, Serge brings over a decade of industry experience, having worked with global giants like Bayer Crop Science and JBS. His leadership blends strategic insight with servant leadership values—empowering teams, driving innovation, and ensuring operational excellence. Fluent in English, French, Lingala, and Tshiluba, Serge is uniquely positioned to bridge transatlantic business cultures and lead the consortium's mission to strengthen U.S.–DRC industrial ties.

Drew Thompson

Dre Thompson is a veteran real estate developer and founder of Hubert Development, a North Carolina-based firm pioneering wellness-focused multi-family communities near medical and academic hubs. With over 20 years of experience in both residential and commercial development, Dre’s work emphasizes sustainability, functionality, and innovation. As a member of the International WELL Building Institute, Dre applies global wellness standards—Air, Water, Light, Nourishment, Fitness, Comfort, and Mind—to create integrated living environments. His strategic mindset and expertise in infrastructure development add significant value to the consortium's industrial and community-based initiatives in the DRC.

Andrew Riddaugh

Andrew Riddaugh brings a unique background in national governance and private sector innovation to the board. Having served four years in the Trump administration as a Senior Advance Lead within the Office of Presidential Advance, Andrew gained critical experience in tech infrastructure, crisis management, foreign affairs, and strategic communication. He has worked alongside U.S. cabinet officials, members of Congress, foreign dignitaries, and business leaders. As the founder of Liberation, a company grounded in the ideals of “Freedom Tech,” Andrew contributes a forward-looking, security-conscious approach to technology and public-private collaboration within the consortium.
